About Me

Hi, I’m Mohammad Rezaei. I’m a Biomedical Engineer with a passion for understanding the brain and translating that into real‑world solutions. I love diving into neuroscience, neuroimaging, and signal processing, and over the past few years I have:

  • Investigated gray‑matter changes in Alzheimer’s Disease using high‑resolution voxel‑based morphometry;
  • Built spiking‑neuron network simulations (Izhikevich models) to study critical avalanches and bifurcations;
  • Analyzed ECG systems and cardiac electrophysiology circuitry to deepen my grasp of physiological signals;
  • Explored creative decision‑making in soccer players through fmri analyses;
  • Studied motor learning via EEG/fMRI signal analysis coupled with cognitive and behavioral experiments.

I work at the intersection of neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, and signal processing, and I’m excited to keep turning data into discoveries that improve lives.
