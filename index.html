About Me

Hi, I’m Mohammad Rezaei. I’m a Biomedical Engineer with a passion for understanding the brain and translating that into real‑world solutions. Over the past few years I’ve:

  • Applied voxel‑based morphometry to uncover gray‑matter changes in Alzheimer’s Disease;
  • Built spiking‑neuron network simulations using Izhikevich models to study critical avalanches and bifurcations;
  • Analyzed ECG systems and cardiac electrophysiology circuitry to deepen my engineering understanding of physiological signals;
  • Explored decision‑making and motor learning through EEG/ECG signal analysis and behavioral studies.

I thrive at the intersection of neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, and signal processing. I’m especially excited about applying these skills to wearable assistive technologies and rehabilitation devices that help people regain mobility and independence.
