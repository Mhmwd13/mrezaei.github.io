About Me

Hi, I’m Mohammad Rezaei. I’m a Biomedical Engineer with a deep curiosity for the brain’s mysteries and a heart set on helping people move better. Over the past years I’ve:

  • Used high‑resolution voxel‑based morphometry to uncover gray‑matter changes in Alzheimer’s Disease,
  • Built spiking‑neuron network simulations (Izhikevich models) to study critical avalanches and bifurcations,
  • Explored decision‑making and motor learning through EEG/ECG signal analysis and behavioral experiments.

I thrive at the intersection of neuroimaging, computational neuroscience, and signal processing. I’m especially excited about translating these insights into wearable assistive technologies and rehabilitation solutions—helping people regain mobility and independence. I can’t wait to bring my hands‑on experience and collaborative spirit to the MetaMobility Lab’s work on adaptive exoskeletons and personalized rehabilitation devices.

My current work focuses on motor learning and IMU‑based kinematic estimation, and I’m eager to apply these skills to adaptive exoskeleton control and sensor‑fusion pipelines in the MetaMobility Lab.
